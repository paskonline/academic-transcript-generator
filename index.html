To bulk-upload your academic results, your Excel or CSV file must follow this structure:

Headers (Row 1): Year, Semester, Module Name, Result, Credits

  • Module Name & Result: Mandatory fields.
  • Year & Semester: Highly recommended for automatic sorting and year-dividers.
  • Credits: Optional (system defaults to 3 credits if left blank).

What is GPA?

The Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized numerical representation of your academic performance. Instead of looking at individual percentages, the GPA condenses your results into a single figure—typically on a 4.0 scale. It reflects the average of the "grade points" you earn in each course, weighted by the number of credit hours that course carries.

The Importance of GPA

Your GPA is more than just a number; it is a key metric used by educational institutions and employers worldwide to gauge your consistency and dedication.

  • Academic Standing: It determines if you are eligible for Dean’s List honors, scholarships, or specialized research circles.
  • Class Awards: In Sri Lanka, your final GPA determines your degree classification (e.g., First Class, Second Upper, or Second Lower).
  • Postgraduate Opportunities: If you plan to pursue an MBA or a Master’s degree later, your undergraduate GPA is the primary filter used for admissions.

How is GPA Calculated?

The calculation follows a "weighted average" logic. A 4-credit core module affects your GPA more than a 2-credit elective.

GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credits) / Total Credits
  1. Assign Grade Points: Letter grades (A, B, C) are converted to numerical values (e.g., A = 4.0).
  2. Calculate Quality Points: Multiply Grade Points by Credits for each module.
  3. Summation: Add up all Quality Points and attempted Credits.
  4. Division: Divide Total Quality Points by Total Credits.

Why is it Necessary for Us?

As students balancing full-time work, professional exams, and university lectures, tracking your GPA is essential for strategic planning.

  • Goal Setting: Calculate exactly what grades you need in your final year to reach your target classification.
  • Career Readiness: In Finance and Accounting, a strong GPA serves as a "first-look" indicator of your technical discipline.
