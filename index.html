User Guide: How to generate your transcript

Follow these simple steps to create a professional academic record in minutes.

1
Complete your Profile: Fill in your name, university, and degree details. This information appears at the top of your final document.
2
Enter Results: Use the "+ Add New Module Row" button to enter your grades manually, or use the "Import" button to upload an existing file.
3
Automatic Organization: Simply type the academic year (e.g., Year 1) and semester. The system will automatically sort your modules and add professional year-dividers.
4
Download PDF: Once satisfied, click "Download Transcript" to generate a high-quality A4 document ready for printing or sharing.

Learn more about GPA

What is GPA?

The Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized numerical representation of your academic performance. It reflects the average of the "grade points" you earn in each course, weighted by the number of credit hours that course carries.

The Importance of GPA

Your GPA is a key metric used by employers and universities worldwide to gauge consistency. In Sri Lanka, it determines your degree classification (First Class, Second Upper, etc.) and is a primary filter for postgraduate admissions.

How is it Calculated?

The calculation follows a "weighted average" logic. A 4-credit module affects your score more than a 2-credit elective.

GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credits) / Total Credits
