How to Use the Transcript Generator

1. Complete your Profile: Fill in your name, university, and degree details. This information appears at the top of your final document.

2. Change the Grading System: If your university/institution uses a specific grading system, you can change it via the "Grading System" button. Unless you change it, your GPA is calculated using the default system.

3. Enter Results: Use the "+ Add New Module Row" button to enter your grades manually, or use the "Import" button to upload an existing file.

  • Credit: Find the credit for each module by checking your module handbooks or contacting the department/lecturer.
  • Result: Add your result for each module as issued by the examination department of your university.

4. Automatic Organization: Simply type the academic year (e.g., Year 1) and semester. The system will automatically sort your modules and add professional year-dividers.

5. Download PDF: Once satisfied, click "Download Transcript" to generate a high-quality A4 document ready for printing or sharing.

Import Data Instructions

You can bulk-upload your academic results using an Excel (.xlsx, .xls) or CSV file.

⚠️ Compulsory Headers:

Your file MUST include columns named exactly "Module Name" and "Result".

Step 3: Compulsory vs Optional

  • Credits: OPTIONAL. If not provided, the system defaults to 3 credits.

Academic Guide

What is GPA?

The Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized numerical representation of your academic performance, typically on a 4.0 scale.

Why is it important?

In Sri Lanka, your final GPA determines your degree classification (e.g., First Class, Second Upper). It is also a primary filter used for postgraduate admissions and corporate recruitment.

GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credits) / Total Credits
